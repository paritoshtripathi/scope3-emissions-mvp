Scope 3 emissions are indirect greenhouse gas emissions that occur in a company's value chain. These emissions often represent the largest source of emissions for companies, accounting for up to 70% of their carbon footprint.

Common sources of Scope 3 emissions include:
1. Purchased goods and services
2. Business travel
3. Employee commuting
4. Waste disposal
5. Transportation and distribution
6. Use of sold products
7. End-of-life treatment of sold products
8. Investments

Companies can reduce their Scope 3 emissions through:
- Supplier engagement programs
- Sustainable procurement policies
- Remote work policies
- Waste reduction initiatives
- Efficient logistics planning
- Product lifecycle optimization
